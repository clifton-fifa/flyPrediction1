Blow Flies

Chrysomya megacephala (Fabricius)

Description: Chrysomya megacephala, commonly known as the Big-headed Blow Fly, is a species of blow fly found in tropical and subtropical regions. They are characterized by their large head and metallic green to blue coloration.

Chrysomya rufifacies (Macquart)

Description: Known as the Reddish Brown Blow Fly, Chrysomya rufifacies is distinguished by its reddish-brown color and metallic sheen. It is found in various parts of Asia and Australia and is known for its role in forensic entomology.

Chrysomya (Ceylonomyia) nigripes Aubertin

Description: The Black-legged Chrysomya features a distinctive black coloration on its legs and a metallic green body. It is often found in tropical areas and plays a significant role in decomposing organic matter.

Lucilia cuprina (Wiedemann)

Description: Known as the Copper Blow Fly, Lucilia cuprina is characterized by its metallic copper-green coloration. It is commonly found in Australia and is known for causing myiasis in livestock.

Hemipyrellia ligurriens (Wiedemann)

Description: Hemipyrellia ligurriens is a blow fly with a distinctive black body and orange legs. It is prevalent in the Mediterranean region and is involved in the decomposition of organic material.

House Fly

Musca domestica Linnaeus

Description: The House Fly is one of the most common and widespread flies. It has a grayish body with four dark stripes on its thorax. It is known for its role in spreading diseases and its adaptation to living in close association with humans.

Flesh Fly

Parasarcophaga (Liosarcophaga) dux Thomson

Description: The Flesh Fly is distinguished by its gray body and black stripes. This species is commonly found in decomposing animal matter and is known for its role in forensic investigations.
